# FAZLE AI PLATFORM
# PKM -> KE -> KBG PROGRAM
## Board-Ready Executive Version

Version: 3.0 (Executive)

Company: Al-Aqsa Security and Logistics Services Ltd.

---

## 1) Executive Decision Brief
The Fazle production system is currently the living operational brain. Critical business and safety logic is still embedded in source code and not fully represented in the knowledge base.

Board decision required:
- Approve controlled conversion of production knowledge into a governed knowledge base.
- Approve conflict-resolution protocol for policy/formula mismatches.
- Approve visibility model (public vs internal vs confidential).

No production changes were made for this report.

---

## 2) Why This Matters
If hidden logic remains undocumented:
- Operational continuity depends on code-readers, not organizational memory.
- Safety guards can be unintentionally bypassed during future changes.
- Policy and runtime behavior can drift, creating governance and audit risk.

If approved and executed:
- Every critical workflow becomes reproducible from documentation.
- High-risk decisions become traceable and auditable.
- Business continuity improves beyond individual engineers/operators.

---

## 3) What We Found (Board-Level)
### 3.1 Operational Architecture
The platform is an identity-first messaging operations engine covering:
- Recruitment
- Attendance
- Escort lifecycle
- Payment and payroll
- Admin governance
- Scheduler automation
- RAG-based knowledge retrieval

### 3.2 Hidden Critical Logic Exists
High-impact hidden rules identified in production behavior:
- Silent-skip and draft-only routing controls
- Complaint/financial escalation protections
- Prompt-injection and outbound leakage blockers
- Loop/flood suppression controls
- Role-priority identity resolution
- Multi-stage fallback behavior for AI and delivery

### 3.3 Coverage Gap
Current KB provides policy and workflow foundations but under-covers:
- Runtime safety gates
- Full command governance and RBAC mapping
- Parser internals and validation boundaries
- Scheduler and RAG internals

---

## 4) Risk Snapshot
### R1 - Knowledge Concentration Risk
Critical behavior remains trapped in code instead of approved operational documents.

### R2 - Governance Drift Risk
Some policy-level assumptions conflict with runtime formula behavior.

### R3 - Control Surface Risk
Incomplete documentation of admin commands and role permissions increases misuse risk.

### R4 - Safety Regression Risk
Undocumented anti-abuse and outbound safety controls may be weakened in future updates.

---

## 5) Conflict Areas Requiring Management Decision
1. Payment formula alignment across modules and policy docs.
2. Transport/allowance values where code and policy references diverge.
3. Draft-estimate vs final-settlement interpretation for release/payment workflows.

Policy rule:
- No silent overwrite.
- Every conflict must have ID, risk note, decision owner, and revision record.
